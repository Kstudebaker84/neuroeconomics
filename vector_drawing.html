Intertemporal Choice

What does it have to do with serotonin?

Intertemporal choice studies how people make choices about what and how much to do at various points in time, when choices at one time influence the possibilities available at other points in time. These choices have trade off costs and benefits at different points in time.

Economists have analyzed the pleasures and pains resulting from a decision using the discounted utility model, which is studied greatly in economics and psychology.

The neurotransmitter serotonin has been implicated in cognition and mood and is involved in regulating mood, emotion, cognition, and regulation behavior. Serotonin also plays an important part in a variety of behavioral impulse-related disorders, including substance abuse and decisions involving immediate gratification.
